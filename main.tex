\item Given the equation of lines passing through the point \myvec{2 \\ 14}. How many more such lines are there, and why?
\section{solution}
 Since the given point is \myvec{2 \\ 14}\\ the, first equation is 
    \begin{align}
        \vec{x} + \vec{y} = 16
    \end{align}
    \begin{align}
        \Vec{x} + \Vec{y} - 16 =0
    \end{align}\\ Second equation is 
    \begin{align}
        \vec{x}-\vec{y}= -12\\
        \Vec{x}-\Vec{y}+12=0
    \end{align}
    Third equation is 
    \begin{align}
     \vec{y}=7\Vec{x}
     \end{align}
     \begin{align}
         7\Vec{x} - \Vec{y} = 0
     \end{align}
    So, we can find infinite equations because through the one point infinite lines can pass.
